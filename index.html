Hi, I'm Yaoyu Yang. I recently earned my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at University of Washington, where I worked on synthetic biology and lab automation in the Klavins Lab. I was a founding member of UW Biofabrication Center (UW BIOFAB) and worked there as a software engineer leading the workflow development. I'll join Ginkgo Bioworks as a senior software engineer starting in Feb 2017.

I studied automation in my undergraduate at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I was intersted in control theory and its applications in electronic and electrical systems. I began my graduate study in UW EE in fall 2011 and found myself fascinated by the emerging area of synthetic biology where I could apply my knowledge in automation to engineer biological systems, which could potentially lead to disruptive innovations in biotechnology. I joined Klavins Lab since then and have been working on several research projects in synthetic biology with the aim of designing, building, and testing synthetic dynamic gene circuits. I worked greatly on building high quality, reproducible build and test workflows in the Aquarium lab oprating system , which we invented in the Klavins Lab. I also worked as an engineer building commercial solutions at Aquarium.

Outside lab and work, I enjoy reading, making cool apps, and helping with my wife's bridal startup.
